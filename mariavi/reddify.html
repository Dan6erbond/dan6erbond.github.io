ReddifyğŸ”—

Reddify is the Discord bot that intuitively allows you to connect your Reddit account with your Discord and communicates the information with other Mariavi bots.

The idea arose as Banhammer was in the making and ensuring a secure connection to verify a user wasn't trying to highjack a subreddit was important, so instead of building in a simple verification system into Banhammer, Reddify was born.

UsageğŸ”—

By design Reddify is supposed to be very simple. No logging into your account through shady third-party sites, no security codes, just a response to a very specific message from the bot and you're verified. The best part, you'll never need to verify again in any servers using Reddify!

Server managers can invite the Reddify bot with the invite link and configure its settings with the commands found in Admin setup.

Call !verify on Discord and respond to the bot on Reddit to link your accounts!

VerificationğŸ”—

Users of Reddify can connect as many Reddit accounts as they wish to their Discord account. But for the username Reddify will always use the first one you connect, so you'll need to !unverify before you can change your "main" account so to speak.

Connecting a Reddit account to your Discord is very simple, just type !verify [reddit_username] and respond to the PM you receive from the bot account /u/TheReddifyBot with verify. If you do not want to connect the account, or someone else attempted to connect accounts, don't respond to the bot at all or respond with a string that does not contain verify in it.

Admin setupğŸ”—

Admins of a server using Reddify have quite a few options to enable/disable features Reddify has and the following are available:

  • !toggle role: assigns the Verified Redditor role to anyone who has verified their acccount with Reddify. This can be useful for restricting certain parts of a server behind this role.
  • !toggle username: changes the nickname of all the verified users to /u/[reddit_username] which is handy to keep large servers organized and restrict usernames.
  • !toggle nick: enables the !nick [new_nick] command which will change a user's nickname to new_nick /u/[reddit_username] and prioritizes the Reddit username.
